#### This turquoise-haired singer has sung in more than one hundred thousand songs; she is not real.​It all began in 2004 when Yamaha decided to create a virtual instrument that emulates singing.​
#### So they created VOCALOID, a software that loads different voice banks.Fast forward and in comes another company called Crypton Future Media. They wanted to create their own voice banks for Vocaloid, so they recorded the vocalist Saki Fujita doing every possible sound in the Japanese language.​
#### For marketing, they figured that the voice needed a face, so they asked a designer called KEI to design something in turquoise, and and thus Hatsune Miku was born.​And even though they have similar Vocaloid characters, Miku is the single most popular.
